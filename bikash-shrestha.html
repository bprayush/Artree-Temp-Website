Bikash Shrestha (b.1985)

“Art is not entertainment. My artworks needle, take or encourage action around social or political issues” “It is a communicative act which open a conversation” says Bikash Shrestha. His works comment on various aspects of contemporary culture, often quite critically, as in most of his works focus on the misdeeds of political figures, social injustice, or confronted ideologies and institutions of identity-based discrimination and conflicts surrounding the governance of society.

“Culture of Silence”, 2016 is a protest performance which address the Right to Health of every citizen of the country. Similarly the “Enough of Your Lies”, a 35 hours protest performance against the unjust and misdeed of the electricity authority and political figures. A photography based work “The Family Album”, address the global scope of human migration and its personal, social, cultural and political consequences. Shrestha is interested in exploring the role of an artist as practitioner, researcher and curator.
